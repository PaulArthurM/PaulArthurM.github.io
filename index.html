Paul-Arthur Meslin

I am a bioinformatics apprentice in the Puissant Lab and the Genomes and Cell Biology of Diseases unit. I studied bioinformatics between Université de Rouen Normandie and Paris (Gustave Roussy and Institut de Recherche Saint-Louis) .

My main interests are in bioinformatics analysis of omics data and reproducible research. I have a growing interest in the mathematical and statistical aspects of biological data analysis.

All my productions are freely available on GitHub: PaulArthurM and you can contact me at paul-arthur.meslin@etu.univ-rouen.fr

Projects

I am currently working on several projects:
  • PitViper: an user-friendly functional screenings meta-analysis tool

    • Goal: to develop software to make functional screening analysis easy for everyone.

    • This tool must run from a CLI or a graphical interface, be configurable and scalable.
    • Many existing tools for the analysis of the essentiality of genes are added.
    • The results are used to generate notebooks with functions to analyse the results from quality control to the visualisation of final results.
    • Based on Snakemake.
  • A Snakemake pipeline to study genetic instability in Acute Myeloid Leukemia
  • A Snakemake pipeline to integrate transcriptomic and epigenetics data
