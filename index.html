Paul-Arthur Meslin

I am a bioinformatics apprentice in the Puissant Lab and the Genomes and Cell Biology of Diseases unit. I studied bioinformatics between Université de Rouen Normandie and Paris (Gustave Roussy and Institut de Recherche Saint-Louis) between 2018 and 2021.

My main interests are in bioinformatics analysis of omics data and reproducible research. I have a growing interest in the mathematical and statistical aspects of biological data analysis.

All my productions are freely available on GitHub: PaulArthurM and you can contact me at paul-arthur.meslin@etu.univ-rouen.fr

Projects

I am currently working on several projects:

  • PitViper: an user-friendly functional screenings meta-analysis tool

    • I work on software to make functional screening analysis easy-to-use, scalable and reproducible. This project is under active development.

  • A Snakemake pipeline to study genetic instability in Acute Myeloid Leukemia

    • We wanted to study genetic instability from DNA-seq data of tumor versus somatic samples.

  • A Snakemake pipeline to integrate transcriptomic and epigenetics data

    • This pipeline has been developed to integrate epigenomic data (ChIP-seq) with transcriptomic data (RNA-seq) in order to identify genes whose regulation follows a given pattern between experimental conditions.
